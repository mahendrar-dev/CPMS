User Guide

Everything you need to know about backups, exporting data, PIN lock, prescriptions, and what's free vs. paid in CPMS.

Free vs. paid

What's free, and what isn't

Is Google Drive Backup included for free? Premium

No — Backup is the only paid part of CPMS. Everything else (patient records, visit history, prescription PDFs, PIN lock, and CSV export) is completely free with no access code needed. To unlock Backup, message us on WhatsApp or email — we'll discuss pricing and then send you a code to enter inside the app under Settings → Backup & Restore.

Backups & Restore

Keeping your data safe

What happens when I restore a backup?

Restoring completely replaces all current data on the device with the data from that backup — it does not merge. Anything added after the backup was taken will be lost. Take a fresh backup before restoring an older one, in case you need to undo it.

Where are my backups stored?

Backups are saved to a private, hidden folder in your Google Drive account reserved for this app. You won't see these files in your regular Drive file list — that's expected and keeps them secure.

Are backups secure?

Yes. Every backup is encrypted with AES-256 before it leaves your device. Only this app can open it.

What do I do after restoring a backup?

Close and reopen the app so the restored data loads correctly.

Exporting Data (CSV)

Getting your data out

Where does my exported file go, and are photos included?

Export creates a single ZIP file containing your CSV data plus the actual patient photos and attachment files, then opens your phone's share menu. You choose the destination — Google Drive, Email, WhatsApp, or "Save to Files." Nothing is uploaded automatically. Inside the ZIP, photos are organized into a "photos" folder and attachments into an "attachments" folder — the CSV's file columns show the matching path so you can find each one.

Why don't I see patient photos in the CSV?

The CSV only lists the photo's file name as text — it doesn't include the actual image, and Excel/Sheets can't open or link to it from that file name alone. Photos live only on this device. To get the actual images off your phone, use Google Drive Backup (Settings → Backup & Restore) instead — that includes all photos.

App Lock / PIN

Locking the app

What if I forget my PIN?

There is currently no PIN-reset option. You would need to uninstall and reinstall the app, which permanently deletes all local data. Always keep a recent Google Drive backup before enabling or changing your PIN, just in case.

How do I change or turn off my PIN?

Go to Settings → Security. Toggle App Lock off to disable it, or tap "Change PIN" to set a new one.

Clinic Logo & Prescriptions

Branding your prescriptions

Where does my clinic logo appear?

Your uploaded logo appears only on the prescription PDF header. It doesn't change the app's own icon or screens.

How do I share a prescription with a patient?

Open the prescription and tap "Generate & Share PDF." Your phone's share menu will open — pick WhatsApp, Email, or "Save to Files" to deliver it.

Patients & Visits

Managing records

What happens if I delete a patient?

Deleting a patient permanently removes all of their visits, prescriptions, and attachments. This cannot be undone — take a backup first if you're unsure.

General

Good habits

Is my data synced automatically anywhere?

No. All data lives only on this device. Nothing is synced or uploaded automatically. Create a Google Drive backup manually and regularly — weekly is a good habit — from Settings → Google Drive Backup (this is the paid add-on described above).
